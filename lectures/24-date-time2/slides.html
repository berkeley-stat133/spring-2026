How old is Oski?

Oski, the official mascot for UC Berkeley, was debuted on September 27, 1941, during a freshman rally at the Greek Theatre.

a <- today()
b <- ymd("1941-09-27")
class(a)
[1] "Date"
class(b)
[1] "Date"
a - b
Time difference of 30847 days

Durations

Subtraction of two base R Date vectors -> difftime vector.

class(a - b)
[1] "difftime"

A difftime can be recorded in many different units, so better to use lubridate::as.duration() to always record it in seconds.

oski_age_sec <- as.duration(a - b)
class(oski_age_sec)
[1] "Duration"
attr(,"package")
[1] "lubridate"
oski_age_sec
[1] "2665180800s (~84.45 years)"

Constructing a duration

You can make a duration by coercing a Date object or using a constructor function.

dseconds(15)
[1] "15s"
dminutes(10)
[1] "600s (~10 minutes)"
dhours(c(12, 24))
[1] "43200s (~12 hours)" "86400s (~1 days)"  
ddays(0:3)
[1] "0s"                "86400s (~1 days)"  "172800s (~2 days)"
[4] "259200s (~3 days)"
dweeks(3)
[1] "1814400s (~3 weeks)"
dyears(1)
[1] "31557600s (~1 years)"

Periods

In "lubridate", a period is like a duration except it doesn’t have a fixed length in seconds. It tracks human units (day, week) that are responsive to irregularities.

Example period constructors:

seconds(15)
[1] "15S"
minutes(10)
[1] "10M 0S"
hours(c(12, 24))
[1] "12H 0M 0S" "24H 0M 0S"


days(0:3)
[1] "0S"          "1d 0H 0M 0S" "2d 0H 0M 0S" "3d 0H 0M 0S"
weeks(3)
[1] "21d 0H 0M 0S"
years(1)
[1] "1y 0m 0d 0H 0M 0S"

Working with periods

one_am
[1] "2026-03-08 01:00:00 PST"
one_am + days(1)
[1] "2026-03-09 01:00:00 PDT"


What will the following yield?

ymd("2024-01-01") + dyears(1)
[1] "2024-12-31 06:00:00 UTC"
ymd("2024-01-01") + years(1) # accounts for leap year
[1] "2025-01-01"

Time Spans recap

Base R

  • difftime: inconsistent units


lubridate

  • duration: fixed span in seconds

  • period: variable span in human units

  • interval: duration plus a starting point

Time Zones

UTC

Coordinated Universal Time (UTC) is primary global time standard. Successor to GMT.

Offsets

Each time zone has a particular offset from UTC.

  • London: +0
  • New York: -5
  • Kyoto: +9
Sys.timezone() # What R says my timezone is

Changing Time

Two flavors

Change the instant in time.

x1 <- ymd_hms("2024-06-01 12:00:00", tz = "America/New_York")
x1
[1] "2024-06-01 12:00:00 EDT"
force_tz(x1, tzone = "Asia/Tokyo")
[1] "2024-06-01 12:00:00 JST"

Keep the same instant but change the way it’s printed:

with_tz(x1, tzone = "Asia/Tokyo")
[1] "2024-06-02 01:00:00 JST"
